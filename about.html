Our Origin Story

It started in 2020 with a simple observation: while the science behind Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) was advancing rapidly, the data available to food manufacturers was stuck in the past.

Our founder, Adam LaCaria, a Data Engineer living with IBS, realized that navigating the grocery store was a statistical nightmare. He saw a disconnect between the 1 in 7 people worldwide suffering from IBS and the food brands trying to serve them.

FODMAP-Analytics was born from a desire to translate complex digestive health data into actionable insights. We don't just count calories; we analyze fermentable carbohydrates to predict market trends before they hit the shelves.

The Rise of the Low-FODMAP Industry

In 2005, researchers at Monash University in Australia coined the term "FODMAP" (Fermentable Oligosaccharides, Disaccharides, Monosaccharides, and Polyols). What began as clinical research has exploded into a global movement.

The "Free-From" food category is evolving. While the 2010s were dominated by the Gluten-Free boom, the 2020s are defined by Gut Health. The global low-FODMAP market is projected to grow at a CAGR of nearly 11% through 2030.

We believe this isn't just a diet trends; it's a medical necessity for millions. We help companies move beyond "gut feeling" strategies and rely on hard data to create products that are both delicious and digestible.
